### Sat Jun 6 - Build In Public

LinkedIn:

One design choice I am leaning into for Agents League:

Do not hide the agent handoff.

In "Your Company Is the Dungeon," the player can see the Master Narrator decompose the pitch, watch the Strategist/Designer/Marketer take turns, and review each artifact before the quest advances.

That makes the product feel less like a black-box chatbot and more like an observable workflow.

Question for agent builders: what part of your agent's reasoning should users always be able to inspect?

### Sun Jun 7 - Verification Gate

LinkedIn:

The most important part of our Agents League demo is not the sprites.

It is the verification gate.

Every agent-produced artifact stops before it becomes progress. The system validates structure, shows the reasoning trail, and asks the human to approve or reject. Only then does the company earn XP and unlock the next room.

That is the reliability story:

- LLMs create.
- Tools validate.
- Humans approve.
- Replay logs preserve the chain.

This is the pattern I think more agent products need.

Join the Microsoft Reactor session on June 10:
https://aka.ms/AISF/AL-Battle3/Reg
